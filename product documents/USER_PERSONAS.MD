### **Part 1: Detailed User Personas**

Based on the market analysis, these personas represent the primary target segments for Kommon.

### **Persona 1: The International Graduate Student**

**Photo/Avatar:** Headshot of a smiling young woman in her early twenties.

**Name & Demographics:**

- **Name:** Ananya Sharma
- **Age:** 24
- **Major:** M.S. in Computer Science
- **University:** Columbia University
- **Year:** 1st Year Graduate Student

Bio/Background:

Ananya has just arrived in New York from India to begin her master's program. As a "high-value graduate student," her primary focus is on her demanding coursework and she is navigating the city and the U.S. rental process for the first time ("Kommon Market Analysis.pdf"). She represents the "high-need segment" of international students that make up 35% of graduate students at a key institution like NYU ("Market Analysis v2 (Includes TAM).pdf").

**Goals:**

- Find a safe, secure, and affordable apartment close to campus.
- Find a quiet, studious, and reliable roommate.
- Avoid the stress and complexity of the housing search so she can focus on her studies.
- Secure housing without a U.S. co-signer.

**Frictions & Pain Points:**

- **Lack of U.S. Credit History:** She arrives in NYC with "no U.S.-based credit history or guarantor," which is a significant barrier and often a "prerequisite for securing a lease" ("Market Analysis v2 (Includes TAM).pdf").
- **Vulnerability to Scams:** As an international student, she is "disproportionately vulnerable to the pervasive rental scams" such as "phantom rentals" and "ad hijacking" that are common on less secure platforms ("Market Analysis v2 (Includes TAM).pdf").
- **The "Trust Deficit":** She is deeply concerned about the "glaring trust deficit in competitors" like Roomster, which is known for "FTC lawsuits for fake reviews/listings" and "deceptive practices" ("Kommon Market Analysis.pdf").
- **Overwhelming Process:** She is navigating a "complex and restrictive" NYC rental market and is overwhelmed by the "extensive documentation" required, a challenge universities explicitly warn international students about ("Market Analysis v2 (Includes TAM).pdf", "Kommon Market Analysis.pdf").

### **Persona 2: The Out-of-State Undergraduate Student**

**Photo/Avatar:** Photo of a young man in his early twenties, standing on a city street.

**Name & Demographics:**

- **Name:** Ben Carter
- **Age:** 20
- **Major:** B.A. in Film & Television
- **University:** New York University (NYU)
- **Year:** 3rd Year Undergraduate

Bio/Background:

Originally from Florida, Ben has lived in NYU dorms for two years and is now required to find off-campus housing. He is excited about the independence but is facing the intense financial pressure and competition of the NYC market for the first time. He represents the large cohort of "out-of-state students" who must find off-campus accommodation after their initial years ("Market Analysis v2 (Includes TAM).pdf").

**Goals:**

- Find an apartment with 2-3 roommates to make it affordable.
- Live in a vibrant, student-friendly neighborhood.
- Find roommates who are social but also respectful and clean.
- Avoid the stress and inefficiency of the search process.

**Frictions & Pain Points:**

- **Intense Financial Pressure:** With the "average monthly rent for a one-bedroom apartment in Manhattan" at "$4,200," solo living is "untenable." For Ben, finding roommates is a "financial necessity rather than a lifestyle choice" ("Market Analysis v2 (Includes TAM).pdf").
- **Fragmented and Stressful Search:** He is forced to navigate a "disjointed ecosystem of listing sites, social media groups, and messaging apps," a process that is both "inefficient and stressful" ("Kommon Market Analysis.pdf").
- **Frustrating User Experience on Competitor Sites:** On platforms like SpareRoom, he is frustrated by the "time-based paywall" that gates the newest and most desirable listings, creating a "poor user experience" for students on a budget ("Market Analysis v2 (Includes TAM).pdf", "Kommon Market Analysis.pdf").
- **Anxiety Over Compatibility:** He is forced to use the "digital 'Wild West'" of Facebook and Craigslist, which function as "breeding grounds for rental scams" and cause a "high-friction, high-anxiety process" when trying to vet strangers ("Kommon Market Analysis.pdf").

### **Persona 3: The Current NYC Student (First Off-Campus Apartment)**

**Photo/Avatar:** Headshot of a smiling young woman in her early twenties.

**Name & Demographics:**

- **Name:** Chloe Davis
- **Age:** 21
- **Major:** B.A. in Economics
- **University:** Fordham University
- **Year:** 4th Year Undergraduate

Bio/Background:

Chloe grew up in Queens and has lived in Fordham-provided housing. For her senior year, she and a friend want to get an apartment together, but they need to find a trustworthy third roommate to make the rent affordable. She is familiar with NYC but not with the complexities of its rental market.

**Goals:**

- Find a reliable and compatible third roommate to join her and her friend.
- Secure a lease in a safe building without any hidden issues.
- Correctly navigate the legal aspects of lease agreements and security deposits.

**Frictions & Pain Points:**

- **The "Trust and Safety Vacuum":** Her university provides only "passive resource pages" with links and warnings, maintaining a "deliberate distance from the transactional aspects of the housing search" due to liability concerns. This creates a "'trust and safety vacuum'" where she, the student, must navigate the risks alone ("Market Analysis v2 (Includes TAM).pdf").
- **Ineffective University Resources:** The university's off-campus housing portal is little more than an "unvetted listing board" that "lacks the sophisticated matching, verification, and support features" she actually needs ("Kommon Market Analysis.pdf").
- **Fear of Incompatible Strangers:** Her primary fear is the "pervasive risk of...incompatible roommates" which causes significant "housing search anxiety." She needs a way to find someone trustworthy beyond a simple classified ad ("Kommon Market Analysis.pdf").
- **Regulatory Complexity:** She is concerned about correctly navigating NYC's "complex and restrictive" rental market, particularly the laws governing "subleasing, which require landlord consent," and rules around "security deposits" ("Kommon Market Analysis.pdf").
