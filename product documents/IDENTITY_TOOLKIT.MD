Kommon: Identity & Communication Toolkit
1.0 Our Foundation
This is the strategic heart of Kommon, grounding everything we do and say.
1.1 Mission Statement
Our core purpose is to help students create a new home in their new environment and alleviate acculturation stress, improving mental well-being.
1.2 Vision Statement
We envision a world where every student, no matter where they are from, has the foundation to build a true sense of home and belonging.
1.3 Core Values
Our actions are guided by Reciprocity, Trust, and Care. These are structurally embedded through our commitment to the five pillars of Indigenous Systems Thinking: Relational Accountability, Interdependence, Long-View Thinking, Sovereignty as Structure, and Healing-Centered Design.
2.0 Our Voice
This is how we express our identity through language. Our voice is our personality in words.
2.1 Voice & Tone: The Relational Architecture
Our voice is primarily that of a warm, human-centric facilitator. It is inviting, caring, and speaks the language of community ("we," "us," "together"). While warm, our tone is also deliberate and clear when explaining how our system works to ensure safety and fairness. When we guide, we do so as an empathetic and empowering partner, creating a safe space for students to learn and grow.
2.2 Core Messaging Pillars
• Structural Safety: We build intentional systems that create trust and safety. We don't just promise it; our architecture ensures it.
• Community Kinship: We facilitate relationships, not transactions. We exist to help students weave the new social fabric they need to thrive.
• Empowered Journeys: We provide a supportive foundation for students to confidently navigate their own path, celebrating their agency and growth.
2.3 Tagline (Optional)
• Kommon: The Architecture of Belonging.
• Kommon: Find Your Foundation.
3.0 Our Visual Toolkit
This is the visual expression of our identity, designed for flexibility and recognition.
3.1 Core Symbol: The Community Threshold
• Concept: The symbol represents the welcoming entrance to a safe space, where a diverse community can leave their worries behind and belong together.
• Description: An abstract threshold shape, created with a single, organic line, cradles a cluster of three simple, overlapping shapes. These shapes symbolize a diverse community (like shoes and sandals left at the door) entering a shared home.
• Principles for Adaptation: The symbol should be used as a single, cohesive unit. It works best in a solid, monochromatic color from our palette to maintain its simplicity and symbolic power.

3.3 Typography
• Primary Typeface (Headlines): Nunito. A rounded, friendly sans-serif for titles, calls to action, and navigation.
• Secondary Typeface (Body): Lora. An elegant, readable serif for paragraphs, detailed descriptions, and email bodies.
• Usage Guidelines: Use Nunito for friendly, attention-grabbing text. Use Lora for readable, trustworthy, longer-form text.
4.0 Participation & Co-Creation
This toolkit is a starting point, not a set of rigid rules. It is designed to be used, adapted, and grown by the Kommon community.
4.1 An Open Invitation
We invite our partners, community members, and collaborators to use these assets to tell our collective story. This identity belongs to all of us.
4.2 How to Contribute
When creating materials for Kommon, lead with our values. Use the toolkit to ensure your design feels warm, trustworthy, and human. Strive for clarity and a sense of welcome.
